How to complete this review (guidance)

Work through each judgement area, starting with the Expected Standard and working up to Strong. For each one:

  1. Read the statement and use the key questions to frame your evaluation.
  2. Triangulate the suggested evidence (data, documents, stakeholder feedback) — treat it as a guide, not a checklist.
  3. Apply a RAG self-rating:
    • Red — insufficient evidence to meet the standard.
    • Amber — partial evidence; standard likely met but gaps remain.
    • Green — strong, comprehensive evidence supports your judgement.
  4. Write a commentary of up to 150 words, in a formal, objective style, focused on impact and improvement.
  5. Add next steps — specific, actionable, and showing how progress will be monitored.

When all Expected judgement areas are green, refer to the Strong Standard to see what further improvement would require. Use the reference lists below (Urgent Improvement / Needs Attention / Exceptional) to inform the overall grade.
